Dental implants

Dental implants

Dental problems can occur for many different reasons, leading to loss. Loss of teeth leads to serious complications in the jawbones, as well as problems with chewing and appearance, which negatively affects people's lives.

Artificial teeth are designed in the form of screws and roots and are used in the treatment of tooth loss. These teeth resemble the shape of the roots of natural teeth and do the same job. Contrary to what is known, the part that is placed in the jawbones is called the "implanted tooth." Artificial teeth are implanted into the jawbones through surgery, thus filling the gap caused by tooth loss. The procedure can also be performed after tooth extraction.

Types of dental implants

Dental implants are divided into two types, according to the region where the implants will be performed: Endosteal implant: During this method, the roots are placed directly into the jawbones, and one or more teeth can be transplanted. The roots are in the form of small conical or cylindrical screws. Subperiosteal implant: In this technique a metal base is attached to the tissue of the jaw bone or gum. Artificial roots are implanted to support the gums, simulating the function of actual dental roots. Dental implants are performed more within the bone than under the bone, only if the jaw bones do not tolerate dental implants then this method is used. The method of transplantation is chosen according to the doctor's vision and the patient's need. In addition, the operation can be performed on one tooth or on the entire mouth. The operation is completed in one day when the dental implants are in the entire mouth, and the results of the dental implant continue throughout the life.
